I graduated from Galvanize Data Science Immersion program focusing on machine learning, inferential statistics, data analysis, and data engineering. I look forward to leveraging my analytical/finance skills with data science at an innovative, mission-driven organization.

Because of ubiquitous data, advanced algorithms, and increasing computing power, machine learning can empower organizations or individuals to make better informed decisions in business, government, and academia. With this belief, I am volunteering at Code for San Francisco, an organization which provides open-source data solutions for Government and their constituents. I completed a predictive model for predicting traffic fatalities that was used to help explain a surge in incidents observed by the Department of Transportation (DOT). I am currently developing a data pipeline and a classification model to predict San Francisco fire incidents. These results may be used to lower fire incidents by optimizing fire inspections efforts.

I received an MBA in Finance from Rice University and a BS in Chemical Engineering from Stanford University. I have applied my analytical skills to solve problems in various industries, including Energy, Renewable Energy, Finance, and Risk Management.
